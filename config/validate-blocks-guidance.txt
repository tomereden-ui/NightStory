Review each script block's spoken text.

A single block can have more than one issue at once — e.g. a phrase that's too intense for the age AND an
unrelated typo elsewhere in the same line. These are two independent checks that must BOTH run on every
block, in this order:
  Step 1 — Content check: is anything too intense, too complex, or off-lesson for age {{AGE}}?
  Step 2 — Proofread check: re-read the block word by word, purely for spelling/grammar, IGNORING content
  entirely this time — even words untouched by step 1 need this pass. A single missing or swapped letter
  that still looks like a plausible word is the easiest thing to miss; look for it deliberately.
If either step finds something, your rewrite must fix everything found across BOTH steps, not just whichever
one you noticed first.

For each block:
- If it is age-appropriate for age {{AGE}}, aligns with the story lessons, and free of typos/grammar errors → it needs nothing; do NOT include it in your response
- If it has one or more issues (slightly scary word, vocabulary too complex for age {{AGE}}, conflicts with lessons, a typo, or a medium-to-high severity grammar error) → rewrite the spoken text to fix ALL of them together and include it with status "fixed"

Rules for age {{AGE}}:
- No death, violence, or genuine fear
- Simple vocabulary a {{AGE}}-year-old understands
- Only gentle tension that resolves warmly
- Reinforce the story's lessons where natural

Rules for typos/grammar:
- Fix genuine misspellings, wrong word choices, subject-verb agreement errors, and missing or duplicated words.
- Do NOT flag or change intentional stylistic choices — short sentence fragments, exclamations, playful
  onomatopoeia (POP, WHOOSH), or a character's own speech quirks are not grammar errors.
- Only fix what a careful proofreader would call clearly wrong, never a stylistic preference.
